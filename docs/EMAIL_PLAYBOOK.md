# Email Campaign Playbook

Rules and structure for creating cold outreach campaigns. Every campaign must follow these guidelines to maintain deliverability, avoid spam filters, and maximize reply rates.

---

## Email Structure Rules

### The 3-Block Rule

Every Step 1 email has exactly 3 blocks:

1. **Lead with relevance** — Personalized opener + pain point. This is the "why you, why now" section. Must reference something specific about the recipient's company or situation. This goes FIRST because the reader decides in the first 2 sentences whether to keep reading.

2. **The meta reveal** — Transparent explanation that this email was generated by the system you're selling. Comes AFTER personalization so the reader first thinks "they know about my company" and then learns it was automated — which makes the demo more impressive. Keep it to 2-3 sentences max.

3. **CTA** — One soft question. No booking links in Step 1. No "let me know if you'd like to chat" filler.

### Follow-up Emails (Steps 2-4)

- Step 2 (Day 2-3): New angle or value-add. Short. No repeat of Step 1 pitch.
- Step 3 (Day 5-7): Social proof, insight, or provocative question. Shortest email in the sequence.
- Step 4 (Day 10-14): Breakup. 2-3 sentences max. Leave the door open.

### Length Limits

- Step 1: Max 80 words in the static template (excluding personalized variables)
- Steps 2-3: Max 60 words
- Step 4: Max 30 words
- Total with variables filled in should never exceed ~120 words for Step 1

## Personalization Guidelines

### What Claude Generates (per lead)

| Variable | Purpose | Word Limit | Rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| `personalized_opener` | Why we're emailing THIS company | 40 words | Must reference a concrete detail: project types, market focus, team structure, tech, hiring signals. NEVER "I came across" or "I was impressed by." |
| `specific_pain_point` | Why a system like ours helps THEM | 40 words | Anchored in their vertical/ICP pain. Must mention their specific buyer type or situation. |
| `signal_hook` | Why NOW (B2B/EU only) | 30 words | References the hiring/scaling signal. Used as the opening line. |
| `suggested_subject` | Unique subject line | 8 words max | Must be specific to this company. No patterns that repeat across all leads. |
| `industry_specific_insight` | Value-add for follow-ups | 30 words | A non-obvious observation about BD in their sub-sector. |

### Claude Prompt Rules

- NO generic phrases: "I came across", "I was impressed by", "As a company that", "It's clear that", "I noticed your company"
- NO filler: "In today's competitive landscape", "With the current market", "As you know"
- Reference CONCRETE details: specific services, project types, markets, technologies, team size, hiring activity
- Use industry-specific language (see per-campaign sections below)
- If Apollo data is thin (no description, no keywords), use company name + industry + location to craft something brief but real — better to say less than to say something generic

### Subject Lines

- ALWAYS use Claude-generated `suggested_subject` as the primary subject
- Hardcoded pattern subjects (e.g., "New project pipeline for {{Company}}") go as B/C variants only
- Subject must be specific enough that it couldn't apply to 100 other companies
- Max 8 words
- No spam trigger words: "free", "guaranteed", "limited time", "act now", "opportunity"
- No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation

## The Meta Line

The meta angle (showing the email is automated as a product demo) is core to the pitch. But it must not trigger spam filters or feel robotic.

### Rules

- The meta line goes AFTER personalization, never before
- Frame it as transparency, not a brag: "Full transparency —" not "Here's the thing:"
- NEVER use: "wrote itself", "found you automatically", "this email found [company]"
- DO use: "this email was sourced, written, and sent by the system I sell" or "I built a system that finds firms like yours, researches them, and writes relevant intros — you're reading the output"
- Keep it to 2 sentences max
- It should do double duty: prove the system works AND explain what you sell

### Per-Campaign Meta Lines

**AEC:**
> Full transparency — I built a system that finds firms like yours, researches them, and writes relevant intros. You're reading the output. This is exactly what I set up for engineering and architecture firms who want a steady pipeline without pulling principals away from delivery.

**B2B / EU:**
> Full transparency — this email was sourced, written, and sent by the system I sell. No SDR, no VA, no manual work. I build these for B2B teams who want more pipeline without more headcount.

## CTA Guidelines

### Step 1 CTA
- Soft question, not a booking link
- Good: "Is this something that makes at all sense right now?"
- Good: "Open to a quick chat about how this would work for {{companyname}}?"
- Bad: "Grab 20 minutes here: [link]" (too aggressive for Step 1)
- Bad: "Would you be open to a brief call to discuss this further?" (most overused CTA in cold email)
- Bad: "Let me know if you'd like to learn more" (passive, no question to answer)

### Step 2+ CTA
- Can include a booking link
- Can be more direct: "Does [specific day] work for a 15-min call?"
- Breakup email: no CTA, just leave the door open

## Spam Avoidance

### Structural Rules
- No links in Step 1 (except signature — and even that's optional)
- No images
- No HTML formatting (bold, colors, etc.)
- No tracking pixels if possible
- Plain text only

### Content Rules
- No identical sentences across all recipients — the meta line and pitch should have slight variations via A/B/C variants
- Subject lines must vary per recipient (Claude-generated)
- Avoid words that trigger filters: "free", "guaranteed", "no obligation", "act now", "limited", "exclusive"
- Don't reference automation/bots/AI in the subject line
- Keep emails short — long cold emails get flagged

### Sending Rules
- Warm up accounts for 2+ weeks before sending at volume
- Max 40 new emails/day per account during ramp
- Randomize send times within the send window
- Use 2+ subject variants per step (A/B test)
- Space emails 2-3 minutes apart minimum

## Checklist: Before Launching a Campaign

- [ ] Email accounts warmed up 2+ weeks
- [ ] At least 2 subject variants for Step 1
- [ ] Step 1 body under 120 words (including variables)
- [ ] No links in Step 1 body
- [ ] Meta line comes AFTER personalization
- [ ] `suggested_subject` used as primary subject (not hardcoded pattern)
- [ ] Claude prompt tested with 5+ sample leads — outputs feel specific, not generic
- [ ] Instantly spam checker shows no red flags
- [ ] Send schedule set (weekdays only, business hours in recipient timezone)
- [ ] Daily send limit configured per account
